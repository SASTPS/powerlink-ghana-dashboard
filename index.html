Dashboard Purpose

PowerLink Ghana 2050 is a student-designed scenario for a hybrid energy hub in Ghana. The system combines renewable generation, battery storage, flexible backup power, future grid integration, productive agriculture, circular waste-to-value systems, and community training.

Hybrid energy + agriculture + circular economy

This dashboard helps explain how PowerLink Ghana could improve reliability, affordability, equity, climate impact, health/safety, and long-term resilience.

Real-World Ghana Signals

  • Ghana already has related waste-to-energy and circular bioeconomy activity.
  • Organic waste can be converted into biogas, electricity, and fertilizer.
  • Cocoa pod husks, crop residues, and market waste can support compost, biochar, or biogas pathways.
  • Ghana’s carbon market and Article 6 activity create a stronger climate-finance context.
